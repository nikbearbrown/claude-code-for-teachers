## 3. Application Domain Examples

(Domain coverage map: Ch 14 sits in simulation + book retrospective.)

Teacher's post-build document, five sections (worked example for sorting-algorithm simulation):

1. **What I built (plain language):** "An interactive visualization of bubble sort that shows why it's O(n²). Students step through comparisons; the simulation highlights what's being compared, what's being moved, and the growing sorted region. Deployed via Claude Artifacts; embedded in the class website."
2. **What I delegated to Claude and why:** "The HTML scaffold and CSS layout — pattern work Claude does faster. The bubble-sort logic itself — well-specified algorithm with clear test cases."
3. **What I kept for myself and why:** "The Intent Layer (what students should understand); the DESIGN.md decisions about color and interaction; the decision to teach bubble sort *as a counterexample to efficiency*, not as a tool to memorize."
4. **What I learned that I didn't know before:** "Claude can iterate on visual output if I give it a screenshot to compare against. The DESIGN.md six-color constraint was the most useful thing I did — it forced me to decide what the simulation was *for* before any color got chosen."
5. **What I would do differently:** "Start with the Intent Layer earlier — I drafted it after CLAUDE.md and DESIGN.md, and it changed what I would have put in those. Reverse the order next time. Also: the step-controls in Step 4 should have been specified before the simulation logic in Step 3 — I rebuilt Step 3 because the logic didn't match the controls."

The chapter walks through with the teacher's voice; honesty is the discipline.

## 6. Pedagogical Delivery Research

### Prior knowledge required
The teacher has finished the book.

### Common misconceptions to disarm
- **"Post-build is bureaucratic."** Five sections; <30 minutes.
- **"I'll write it when I have time."** Now. Apply-level exercise: this document, today.
- **"My document should be modest."** Performative humility is failure mode.

### Effective instructional sequences
- **Walk through the five sections.** TIKTOC names.
- **Show Seth's or author's post-build document.** Concrete example.
- **The reader writes their own.** Apply-level exercise. Last instruction.

### Known failure modes
- **The chapter as ceremony.** Portable artifact, not duty.
- **Over-claiming "you're done."** Forward momentum without preaching.
- **The book's voice continuing too long.** Book ends; teacher is now practitioner.

### What separates understanding from memorization
A reader who *understands* Ch 14 has produced a post-build document with a specific "what I would do differently" entry that names a real decision they would reverse. A reader who memorized Ch 14 produces a generic document.
