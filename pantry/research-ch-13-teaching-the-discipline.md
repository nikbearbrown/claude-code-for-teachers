## 2. The Core Concept — State of the Field

### What is settled

- **PCK is real and identifiable.** Education research since Shulman 1986.
- **Teacher-built artifacts have pedagogical value beyond their use.** Constructionism (Papert).
- **Process assessment measures different things than product assessment.** Established.
- **Specification-writing is emerging as a core CS literacy.** Challen 2026; broader 2025–2026 CS-education conversation.

### What is disputed

- **Whether build logs are practical at K-12 scale.** Heavier than product review; pedagogically richer.
- **Whether to grade build logs.** Open. Author should give options.
- **Whether teacher artifacts should be shared with students verbatim.** TIKTOC implies yes; the chapter should give redaction guidance.

### What has changed recently (last 5 years)

- 2024–2026 rise of AI-assisted student work has forced new assessment practices. Build logs are one emerging answer.
- Challen's January 2026 talk is the most current statement of "specification-writing as CS literacy."
- The Anthropic Education Report's "teachers building custom tools" finding validates the teacher-as-builder pattern at scale.

## 6. Pedagogical Delivery Research

### Prior knowledge required
The teacher has finished the book's three builds.

### Common misconceptions to disarm
- **"Teaching is just assigning the students book."** No.
- **"My artifacts are too personal."** With redaction, most are teaching material.
- **"Build logs are too much work."** Peer review reduces teacher load.
- **"Students won't see my framework as authority."** They will if you've lived it.

### Effective instructional sequences
- **The chapter map table.** 14 rows; ongoing reference.
- **The teacher's CLAUDE.md as classroom walkthrough.** Apply-level exercise.
- **The build-log assessment design.** Apply-level exercise.
- **The teacher's role during student builds.** Concrete questions.

### Known failure modes
- **The chapter as students-book-summary.** It's a map, not a summary.
- **Over-claiming the teacher's CLAUDE.md authority.** One teacher's lessons; model not sacred text.
- **Build-log fetishism.** One assessment tool among options.

### What separates understanding from memorization
A reader who *understands* Ch 13 has prepared a redacted version of their CLAUDE.md for sharing and designed at least one build-log assessment. A reader who memorized Ch 13 can recite the chapter-map table without using it.
