## 2. The Core Concept — State of the Field

### What is settled

- **Constructionism produces deeper learning than passive consumption.** Education research (Papert, Resnick, Kafai).
- **Tools designed by teachers for their own classrooms transfer better than top-down tools.** CS-education research (CSTA, NCWIT data).
- **The shift from "saves time" to "enables possibility" is real.** Teachers who reach Act Three's level describe a qualitative shift.

### What is disputed

- **Whether all teachers can reach this shift.** Book argues yes with discipline; some practitioners argue most stop at Act Two.
- **Whether the simulation is the right Act Three example.** TIKTOC's OQ-1 names this as load-bearing unresolved.

### What has changed recently (last 5 years)

- 2024–2026 agentic tools make "tools that enable new classroom experiences" more accessible than 2022.
- Claude Code's three-file system (Brutalist) is the operational discipline that makes the ambition tractable.

## 4. The Book's Thesis Connection

The Bridge is the motivation shift. Acts One and Two were about the teacher's work better. Act Three is about pedagogical work that wasn't possible before.

The Bridge must:
1. **Narrate the shift.** Seth's voice. Reader recognizes from their own building.
2. **Set up Act Three's stakes.** Simulation serves the student. Build risk is different.
3. **Pose the teacher's Act Three question.** TIKTOC: "What concept in your curriculum has no good interactive analog yet?"

## 6. Pedagogical Delivery Research

### Prior knowledge required
Act One + Act Two fully experienced.

### Common misconceptions to disarm
- **"Act Three is optional."** Without it, the framework remains a productivity tool.
- **"The simulation is too ambitious."** Scaled to a real classroom; deployable this school year.
- **"I don't have a 'simulation' to launch."** Reframe: any interactive classroom experience that requires complex setup is a candidate.

### Effective instructional sequences
- **Seth's voice.** Bridge chapters work in first person.
- **One concrete moment.** Seth's specific "this changed everything" instance.
- **The teacher's Act Three question.** TIKTOC's posed question is the exit. Don't answer; pose.

### Known failure modes
- **Motivational poster.** Specific, not aspirational.
- **Instructional.** Bridge is narrative; no exercises.
- **Over-long.** 1,500–2,500 words in the book's body.
